Visualizing the Transient Sky: Real-Time All-Sky Imaging and Open Data in the TART Ecosystem
The transformation of cosmic signals into visual representations of the radio sky is the cornerstone of the Transient Array Radio Telescope (TART), a low-cost array radio telescope installed recently at Independent University, Bangladesh (IUB) by Tim Molteno and others. This abstract explores the software pipeline that converts raw voltage correlations into all-sky images, a process central to the recent workshops at IUB. By leveraging a decentralized, open-source computational framework, TART enables real-time synthesis imaging without the need for high-cost proprietary software. This approach provides a powerful pedagogical tool for understanding radio interferometry while establishing a scalable model for global monitoring of the transient L1-band radio universe through collaborative digital innovation.
Processing raw visibilities—the complex spatial frequencies captured by the 24-antenna array—is the core of the imaging pipeline. Workshop participants utilized the Radio Interferometer Measurement Equation (RIME) to model the signal path, accounting for antenna gains and phase offsets. Through standard calibration routines, these raw visibilities are transformed into a stable data stream ready for Fourier synthesis. We demonstrate the use of imaging algorithms and containerized pipelines, such as Stimela, to produce continuous snapshots of the radio sky. This end-to-end digital workflow proves that complex astronomical data reduction is achievable on consumer-grade hardware, making advanced radio astronomy education globally accessible.
The TART ecosystem is uniquely defined by its commitment to open data, exemplified by the live web-based monitoring system at map.elec.ac.nz. This interface provides an interactive, real-time window into telescope operations, displaying live all-sky maps and visibility diagnostics to a global audience. For the IUB TART, this platform serves as both a public outreach tool and a diagnostic hub, allowing researchers to observe transient events and satellite passes instantaneously from any location. By integrating live telemetry with open-source software, the TART project invites institutions to join a distributed network of observers, democratizing astronomical discovery through radical transparency and global accessibility.
